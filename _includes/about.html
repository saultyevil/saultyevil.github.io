I am a PhD student researching optical and UV line formation in Tidal Disruption Events, using the Monte Carlo radiative transfer and ionization code PYTHON to to model the ionized outflows of TDEs. I'm currently at the Department of Physics and Astrophysics at the University of Southampton under the supervsion of Christian Knigge. At Southampton, I'm also part of the Computational Modelling Group and the Next Generational Computational Modelling Centre for Doctoral Training (NGCM-CDT). I completed my integrated masters in physics with astrophysics at the University of Kent, finishing with first class with honours. For my bachelor's disseration, I created a script to search for High Proper Motion stars and Brown Dwarfs in the UKIDSS-GPS and UWISH2 survey data. My masters thesis, on the other hand, was more theoretically focused on where I conducted hydrodynamics simulations of extragalactic jets using the astrophysical fluid dynamics code PLUTO.

During the course of my PhD, I have attended both national and international workshops and conferences, sharing my work with the wider astronomy community. With the NGCM, I have had the opportunity to work with world-leading HPC facilities using, and training in, a wide range of programming languages and parallelisation/HPC frameworks, such as, but not limited to, CUDA, OpenMP and MPI.
