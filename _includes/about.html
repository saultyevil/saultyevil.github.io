I'm a PhD student currently researching optical and UV line formation in Tidal Disruption Events, using the Monte Carlo radiative transfer and ionization code PYTHON to model ionized outflows in TDEs. I'm currently part of the Department of Physics and Astrophysics at the University of Southampton, under the supervsion of Professor Christian Knigge. I'm also part of the Computational Modelling Group and the Next Generational Computational Modelling Centre for Doctoral Training (NGCM-CDT) at Southampton. I completed my integrated masters in physics with astrophysics at the University of Kent, finishing first class with honours.

During my PhD, I have had the opportunity to attend both national and international workshops and conferences, enabling me to share my work with the wider community. With the NGCM, I've been able to work with world-class HPC facilities using, and training in, a wide range of programming languages and parallelisation/HPC frameworks, such as, but not limited to, CUDA, OpenMP and MPI.

My bachelor's disseration focused on identifying high proper motion stars, where I created a script to process candidate objects in UKIDSS-GPS and UWISH2 survey data. My masters thesis, was more theoretical and centred on hydrodynamic simulationsof extragalactic jets, using the astrophysical fluid dynamics code PLUTO.
