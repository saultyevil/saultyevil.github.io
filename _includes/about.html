I am a PhD student researching line formation in Tidal Disruption Events using Monte Carlo radiative transfer and ionization simulation, modelling their ionized outflows in. I am conducting my research at the University of Southampton under the supervsion of Christian Knigge. At Southampton, I'm part of the Computational Modelling Group and the Next Generation Computational Modelling Centre for Doctoral Training (NGCM-CDT). I completed my integrated masters in physics at the University of Kent, finishing first class with honours. For my bachelor's disseration, I created a simple script to search for High Proper Motion stars in the UKIDSS-GPS and UWISH2 survey data. My masters thesis focused on inviscid hydrodynamics simulations of extragalactic jets using PLUTO.

During the course of my PhD, I have attended both national and international workshops and conferences, sharing my work with the wider astronomy community. With the NGCM, I have had the opportunity to work with world-leading HPC facilities using, and training in, a wide range of programming languages and parallelisation/HPC frameworks, such as, but not limited to, CUDA, OpenMP and MPI.
